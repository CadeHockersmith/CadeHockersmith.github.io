Historical Narrative | Junior Year

Project Reflection:

This assignment required students to create a narrative that effectively implemented historical details. The narrative needed to convey a historical event through the use of dialogue and character development. The reader should be able to learn more about the chosen event without feeling like they read a research paper. The main focus of the assignment was to create a coherent plot that consistently progressed the theme of the story.

Project Reflection:

This project improved my ability to create effective dialogue that conveyed character details. Previously, I was unable to implement dialogue that brought the story forward which resulted in a story that relied solely on setting details. I learned how to implement dialogue that motivated the reader to stay interested throughout the text. The project explained the best way to move through the plot without slowing down for unnecessary information. Overall, this project greatly improved my ability to build an interesting world within a narrative.

Flash Talk: The Internet and Society | Junior Year

Project Reflection:

This project required students to identify a topic related to the Internet and its impact on people and society. The purpose of this flash talk was to demonstrate my knowledge of the Internet and how it works by connecting the information to a relevant topic. The previous lessons covered the technology behind digital security, so the potential topics referenced this concept. The end goal of the assignment was to summarize a complicated issue into an easily understandable presentation.

Project Reflection:

I chose to research the topic of Internet Censorship as it continued to be relevant within the news. Protecting digital freedom is a vital aspect of ensuring the growth of the United States. I believe that myself and my peers should be educated in understanding the potential flaws of the Internet. By identifying common misconceptions about Internet Censorship, my peers were able to better understand this topic. Overall, I was able to improve my presentation skills by summarizing the information researched within a small speech

Narrative Essay | Junior Year

Project Explanation:

This project required me to identify a severe issue in modern day society while offering a solution that would be accepted by both sides of the issue. The topic was open ended to encourage students to choose an issue that they could passionately identify with. The points made must be supported with strong evidence that accurately identifies the problem. This was a useful way to revisit MLA citations in a relevant project. Students with opposing beliefs were encouraged to participate, so the end solution would represent both sides equally.

Project Reflection:

My topic, Internet Censorship, motivated me to research more about its implementation nationwide. Prior to my research, I completely refused to accept the Internet Censorship should be utilized in the modern age. However, evidence referenced by those with opposing viewpoints offered defendable evidence that resulted in me understanding their beliefs. This project taught me how to accept audience feedback when arguing a solution. Persuasive writing skills are an incredibly valuable asset for communicative works. Overall, this project contributed to my ability to successfully present information in future assignments.

Connecting Music to Elie Wiesel's Night | Sophomore Year

Project Explanation:

This assignment required me to connect the Novel Night to a song of our choosing. We were taught how to identify similarities between different mediums. Our ability to write an expository essay was tested here. This project introduced me to the concepts of creating an interesting essay that maintained a theme. The assignment focused on educating students on the proper writing process to create an expository essay.

Project Reflection:

I learned how to properly cite included evidence in an expository essay. In addition, I improved my sentence structure and transitions with this project. I learned how to write an effective essay that informed the reader about a subject through the use of relevant quotations. These writing skills ensured that I am able to identify valuable evidence while researching. Overall, this essay taught me the basics of analyzing sources to obtain new information.
