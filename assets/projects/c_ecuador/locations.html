What a sight!

Ecuador is no exception when it comes to sight seeing. Locations here are beautiful and worthy of a visit..

Ecuador is located in the northwestern region of South America. It is bordered by the Pacific Ocean to the west, Colombia to the north, and Peru to the east and south. The territory of Ecuador consists of the mainland and the Galapagos Islands located 1000km to its west in the Pacific Ocean.

The country is defined by four geographical regions namely La Costa, La Sierra, La Amazonia, and La Region Insular. La Costa makes up the coastal region, La Sierra consists of the Highlands, and La Amazonia making up the eastern part of the country. La Region Insular consists of the Galapagos Islands.

Cotopaxi National Park is located in the Cotopaxi region of Ecuador. This national park is about 60 km from the capital and is home so some of the largest volcanoes, mountains and wildlife. This Ecuadorian national park encompasses an area of 33,393 ha and extends into the Cotopaxi, Pichincha, and Napo provinces. The park is also well known for the four volcanoes within its bounds. Located beside Cotopaxi National Park is the El Boliche National Recreational Area. This stunning park possesses both natural wonders and a fascinating history.
